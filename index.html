Questions You're Probably Asking

the quick answers. before the ask.

when does it launch?

Late 2026. Waitlist is open; Founder's Price locks in for the first 500 names.

is there sugar?

No added sugar. The sweetness comes from xylitol (the chew's structural base). Fruit-forward flavor with no crash.

is it vegan / gluten-free?

Gluten-free, yes. Astaxanthin is sourced as AstaZine® (organic-certified microalgae). Phytoceramides are sourced as Ceratiq® — French non-GMO wheat, tested per batch for gluten-free conformity. The gelling system is under formulation review — vegan status will be confirmed on label at launch.

when should I take it?

On your time. Every day, whenever fits your rhythm. We don't do prescriptive timing — that's how routines die.

where does the clinical evidence live?

Every active is at its published RCT dose. Rather than running our own in-house trial, we let the habit metrics do the talking — 70%+ daily-use by month 3 is the number that proves the product works (the Grüns $1.2B playbook). Every branded ingredient carries its own peer-reviewed evidence at the dose we use.

how long until I feel something?

The chew itself is instant — you feel the 90-second ritual from day one. The substrate-level changes (skin hydration, barrier function for glow) are 30-90 day windows, same as any clinically studied oral ingredient. That's why we designed the ritual around the daily act first: consistency is how the biology gets to do its work.

why postbiotics in guard (not probiotics)?

Most gut products are live probiotics — bacteria you swallow, hoping they survive stomach acid, reach your gut, and stay viable long enough to do something. Viability is fragile: shelf-stability drops, dose is inconsistent, outcomes are hard to predict. Immuno-LP20® is a postbiotic — heat-killed Lactobacillus paracasei. The bacteria aren't alive; the bioactive cell-wall components that engage your gut's immune signaling are preserved. Dose is guaranteed. Shelf-stability is predictable. More consistent inputs, more consistent effects.
